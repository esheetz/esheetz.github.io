During my time at Monmouth College, I completed the Mathematics major, Computer Science major, Spanish minor, and Honors Program. Noteworthy coursework for each is described below.

Mathematics Major

  • Capstone: Evaluating Horror Text Generated by Probabilistic Language Models
  • Independent Research

Computer Science Major

  • Capstone: Writing Horror Text Using Generative Adversarial Networks with Memory
  • Artificial Intelligence

    • As the final project in my artificial intelligence course, I wrote a literature survey that contextualizes Shane Legg's doctoral dissertation Machine Super Intelligence in the research about general artificial intelligence.

Spanish Minor

  • Spanish Peninsular Literature

    • Throughout the semester, I wrote several analytical papers on some of the literature we read in class, including El tragaluz, La vida es sueño, and La de Bringas.

Honors Program

  • Capstone: Chaos Theory and Free Will
  • Words that Changed the World

    • Throughout this course, we examined documents that affected the United States. For the final project, I researched how the language of the Declaration of Independence has inspired minority groups — specifically women and African Americans — to claim freedom and equality for themselves. My project is documented in my poster presentation and final paper.
