Monmouth College

University of Arizona

CAT Vehicle REU

I spent the summer of 2016 at the University of Arizona in Tucson, Arizona working with the Cognitive Autonomous Test (CAT) Vehicle REU. My research partner and I used system identification methods to create a mathematical model to represent the internal dynamics of the CAT Vehicle's controller. By the end of the ten week program, we had found a model for the velocity controller in the vehicle and demonstrated our model on a Proportional Integral Derivative (PID) controller. Our research is explained in our paper draft. As part of the program, my fellow REU participants and I put together videos to describe our experiences. My video is embedded below.

Auburn University

REU on Smart UAVs

I spent the summer of 2017 at Auburn University in Auburn, Alabama working at the REU for Smart Unmanned Aerial Vehicles (UAVs). My research partner and I took a unique approach to the problem of visual homing by combining techniques from previous works. The route of the UAV's exploratory journey away from home was represented by snapshots, turning the problem of long-range visual homing into visiting a series of local waypoints. We trained a convolutional neural network to estimate the homography between reference snapshots and camera images of the return journey. Using the homography, we could compute the direction vector for the UAV to navigate home. Our research is explained in our paper draft and our final presentation.
